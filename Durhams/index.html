Durham's Great Outdoors

This is your bite-size guide to what to get up to, here in this picturesque patch of northeast England. Whether you’re into walks, wheels, water or all three, we’ve got you covered.

TOP Durham
outdoor adventures

There’s more to Durham than Unesco-listed cathedrals and castles. Designated Areas of Outstanding Natural Beauty (the Durham Dales and North Pennines), for example. It’s up to you how you experience Durham’s spectacular tapestry of woodlands, waterfalls, moorlands, forests, fields, farms, beaches and more, but here are some ideas to get you started. (Now might be a good time to eat a pease pudding or two, just to get your energy levels up.)

Set your own pace and take in the county’s incredible views on two feet. Look out for feathered locals (merlin, black grouse, golden plover, curlew, ring ouzel, kingfishers, dippers, buzzards and more) on the Weardale Way, which follows the River Wear. If you prefer waterfalls to wildlife, tick off both Low and High Force falls on the Waterfalls walk in the heart of Teesdale (refuel at the refurbished Bowlees Visitor Centre, conquering cake and tea). For majestic moorland views, stride past upland sheep farms and across country on the Waskerley Way, keeping your eyes peeled for rare, jewel-bright butterflies (especially between May and late autumn). Coastal walks don’t get much more jaw-dropping than the one that takes in Nose’s Point and Denemouth (grab an ice-cream from Lickety Split on the way).

There’s plenty for budding mermen and mermaids to do in Durham. Start by chasing waterfalls (safely) with Kingsway Adventure Centre, which will take you gorge-walking. If you’re feeling brave enough, follow in Ben Fogle’s footsteps and jump off the waterfall, under your guide’s expert instruction. (Don’t worry if you’re not, as the walk includes white-water swimming for liquid thrills.) Learn some piratical skills and flex new-found muscles with the help of Seaham Harbour Marina, whose guides will take you out on the Durham Coast to try kayaking, canoeing, wind-surfing, paddle-boarding and more.

Durham’s varied landscapes are guaranteed to make cyclists’ hearts skip a beat. Let Hamsterley Trailblazers take you on a whirlwind tour of Hamsterley Forest, where around 30 miles of loopy mountain bike trails await. There are different trail-levels marked out amid the evergreens, from blue (beginner) to black (advanced). Hamsterley Trailblazers is a volunteer organisation with a passion for the forest and its bountiful bike thrills; spend an hour or so with one of their guides and you’ll soon see why. Don’t just keep your eyes on the track: the trails come with eye-popping views of Eggleston Moor and the North Pennines Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty (a Unesco European and Global Geopark). Thanks to the stellar efforts of Hamsterley Trailblazers, this forest is only going to get better and better: the group is on a mission to build an extra 2km of track every year. If you’d like to give your legs an easy ride, hire electric bikes and wheel around without tensing a tendon.
