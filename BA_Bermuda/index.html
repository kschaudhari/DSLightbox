This island isn’t designed for squares (triangles, maybe), so pack your adventurous side
Having a bold streak has always come in handy when visiting Bermuda – its first settlers were shipwrecked explorers, after all, and we all know about its famous Triangle. These days, you don’t need to tangle with a coral reef or vanish entirely in order to enjoy the island’s wild side, though a healthy appreciation for being on, and under, water helps.

Wannabe mermaids and mermen are spoiled for choice: go scuba or helmet diving, snorkelling and deep-sea fishing; try stand-up paddle boarding and kayaking; set off on a sunset sailing trip. Jet ski past shipwrecks, coral coves and tiny inlets (keeping your eyes peeled for sea turtles, rays and floundering vessels), swim with dolphins at Dolphin Quest and step into a shark cave at Bermuda Underwater Exploration Institute. In 2017, Bermuda’s turquoise waters will provide a natural amphitheatre for the nautical hijinks of the 35th America Cup.

There’s plenty to please landlubbers, too: the world’s oldest British colony is also a designated UNESCO World Heritage Site. Start by exploring historic Hamilton, admiring the capital’s colonial architecture and cobbled streets. Those iconic red telephone boxes, British-style bobbies and English place names might have you thinking you’re back home, as will the emphasis on afternoon tea (the wonderful weather should provide a firm reminder of your distance from the motherland).
Dock Life, City of Hamilton
Gawp at wildlife at Spittal Pond Nature Reserve (look out for wildflowers, white long tails and even whales), perfect your swing on one of the island’s famous golf courses, hike up Fort Scaur (Bermuda’s highest hilltop) and explore the stalactite-stuffed crystal caves near Castle Harbour. Walk or cycle over the Bermuda Railway Trail, which spans from one end of the island to the other, with the majority of the 18-mile track traversing Bermuda’s scenic northern shore. (You’ll have earned at least three rum swizzles each by the time you’ve ticked off the track’s entirety).

Suits you, sir! World-famous shorts, local designers and dashing dancers
Want to look like an Onion? We don’t mean an actual onion – they’re not stylish. We mean a Bermudian (the locals’ nickname is a humorous nod to the rich onion crop that has historically flourished here).

Bermy style stems from a colourful cultural cocktail. Comfort is key, hence the birth of Bermuda shorts, whose story – surprisingly – begins with tea. During WW1, Britain set up its North Atlantic HQ in Bermuda, meaning a boom in business for teashop owner Nathaniel Coxon. Naval officers thirsty for a good cuppa piled in for their steaming brews, leading to sauna-like temperatures. Nathaniel’s staff complained about the sticky working conditions – their uniform featured a smart navy blazer and khaki trousers – so, rather than fork out on new ones, Nathaniel cannily lopped a few inches off the trouser length.
Shipwrecks to Explore in Bermuda
By cutting them just above the knee, he invented the very first Bermuda shorts. Regular tea-drinker Admiral Mason Berridge was a big fan of the ensemble, so he decided to adopt the style for his fellow officers. Berridge was good enough to acknowledge Coxon for his ingenuity, and Coxon was awarded an OBE. The addition of long socks sealed the deal.

This look is not about flashing a lot of leg – in fact, in the interests of propriety, a law was passed and policemen took to the streets with tape-measures, on the hunt for hussies flashing more than six inches of naked leg. For a more modern take on this traditional short, let the team at TABs help you choose the perfect pair cut for today’s traveller, ideally cut just an inch or two above the knee in a lively colour palette inspired by the island itself (there’s plenty of choice for the ladies, too).
Dock Life, City of Hamilton
Add to your island wardrobe at Hamilton’s Coral Coast Clothing (which donates a portion of every sale to the Bermuda Zoological Society’s charity, Reef Watch). Pick up Bermy bling from local designers: Alexandra Mosher’s pink-sand pendants, triangular pearl earrings from Rebecca Little, or rose-quartz, sapphire and citrine bridal jewellery from DavidRose.

Once the sun sets, show off your new threads and gems at one of Front Street’s famous summer block parties. The town of St George is dotted with boutiques; you can also get your retail kicks at the Bermuda Craft Market, Clocktower Mall and the Dockyard Glassworks.

A final lesson in Bermy style comes from the island’s flamboyant Gombey dancers, whose jewel-bright fringing, tassels and pom-poms pop like fireworks against their milk-white shirts. Imitation is the highest form of flattery…

Fish breakfasts, fish chowder, fish seasons and enough rum to sink a pirate’s ship
In need of a big greeze? (That’s food, to the uninitiated.) Bermuda’s mid-Atlantic location makes for a lip-smacking smorgasbord of culinary influences, including British, American, Caribbean, African and South American.

Let’s start with (codfish) breakfast: a magnificent marriage of boiled cod, buttery potatoes, onions, banana, hard-boiled egg and avocado, usually eaten on Sundays and holidays. Try Bouchee’s version (copy the locals and mash up the components first).

Fish sandwiches are another source of Bermy pride – competitions have even been held to determine the supreme specimen. Consider it a spin on fish and chips: hefty chunks of deep-fried wahoo, slathered in tartar sauce, sandwiched between soft raisin bread or wholemeal toast. Optional extras include onions, tomatoes, lettuce, cheese, coleslaw and hot sauce. Order yours at Art Mel’s Spicy Dicy, paired with ice-cold ginger beer.
Shipwrecks to Explore in Bermuda
Bermuda’s national dish is fish chowder. Fishermen first cooked it hundreds of years ago, simmering their soup in mighty cauldrons above bonfires on the beaches of St George's. The recipe’s essential components remain the same: fish heads (preferably rockfish), tomatoes, thyme, water, root vegetables, rum (Gosling’s Black Seal, natch) and sherry pepper sauce. Like the locals, this chowder can’t be rushed – traditionally, it’s simmered for two and a half days. Wahoo’s Bistro and Lobster Pot both serve chowders that will have you exclaiming ‘Lawdamercy!’

Jot down the 1 September in your diary: it marks the beginning of spiny lobster season. These luscious lobsters come claw-free, meaning more meat in the tail. Crustacean-purists would advise eating them grilled, brushed with butter; you can also try them in tacos, pasta, soups and even sushi.
Dock Life, City of Hamilton
You’ll be thirsty after all that fish, which brings us neatly onto rum. Dark ‘n Stormy ® cocktails and rum swizzles originated in Bermy, starring the island’s Gosling’s Black Seal (affectionately nicknamed ‘Black’). Swizzle Inn is a good place to begin your rum-quest, since it’s the island’s oldest pub. You can also expect to taste rum in cakes and cooking sauces. Yum.

No rose-tinted spectacles needed – Bermy woos with pink sunsets, pink sands and pink hibiscus
If the prospect of 181 islands with aqua waters, blue skies, pink sands and warm weather doesn’t make your heart quicken, you should probably check your pulse. Mother Nature got Bermuda off to a good start – its beautiful beaches have wooed lovebirds for centuries – but there are other reasons why this destination regularly tops honeymoon hotlists. We’ve outlined a lover’s guide to Bermy below; prepare to be seduced.

EAT Have a memorable meal at award-winning Ascot’s Restaurant, which champions the bounty provided by local fishermen.
Shipwrecks to Explore in Bermuda
SEE Sunsets are catnip for courtships. Toast the melting sun from Daniel’s Head Park at Sandy Point. While you’re here, pay a trip to the knee-weakeningly winsome public beach (bring a picnic for maximum smugness); there’s also a little island a short distance from the shore. Early lovebirds can watch the sun rise on St Catherine’s beach and Gates Bay. This stretch of sand has historic significance: it’s where Admiral Sir George Somers and the first colonists washed up after their vessel, the Sea Venture, was scuppered by a coral reef.

PLAY Take your favourite person to Horseshoe Bay in Southampton (ranked at number eight on Tripadvisor’s world’s-best-beaches list). Blush prettily while you’re there to match the Bay’s famous pink sand, which gets its rosy hue from the crushed shells, coral and calcium carbonate that compose it. Other woo-worthy shores include Clearwater Beach, Turtle Bay, Warwick Long Bay, Tobacco Bay and Jobson’s Cove. For a change from sand, try the Hibiscus Garden Trail, which is perfect for fragrant hand-in-hand strolls.
Dock Life, City of Hamilton
STAY Continue la vie en rose at Rosewood Tucker’s Point, which lords it above Castle Harbour and Harrington Sound. Wake up here and you’ll have a holiday’s worth of charms on your doorstep: a private beach, 200 manicured acres, four swimming pools, tennis courts, a duo of restaurants and a rum-toting bar befitting a top-brass admiral. Get lucky in one of the spacious ‘Lucky 17’ Manor House Deluxe rooms (117, 217, 317, 417) and treat yourselves to a couple’s massage at the Senses Spa.
Don’t miss
A couple’s massage in a spectacular natural cave spa at the Grotto Bay Beach Resort & Spa. Only in Bermy…
